The Problem: "Zip Code Justice"
Standard models treat all federal districts as identical. However, some districts are systematically harsher. Because racial demographics vary by district, standard models often mistake a district's harshness for a racial correlation.
The Solution: Multilevel Modeling
By "nesting" cases within their districts, the Multilevel Model separates the local baseline from the individual case factors. It learns to judge the defendant relative to their specific jurisdiction.
The Result
The model stops penalizing defendants for the systemic biases of their location, resulting in significantly fairer predictions without sacrificing accuracy.
